1. Introduction

Welcome to the IT Support Bot! This guide will help you use our automated IT support assistant to get quick answers to your technical questions and create support tickets when needed.

What is the IT Support Bot?

The IT Support Bot is an intelligent assistant that lives inside Microsoft Teams. It's designed to:

  • Answer your IT questions instantly - Get help 24/7 without waiting
  • Provide step-by-step troubleshooting - Follow easy instructions to fix common problems
  • Create support tickets - When you need human IT help, the bot creates a ticket for you
  • Track your requests - Check the status of your support tickets anytime
The bot is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. You can get instant help even outside of normal business hours!

2. Getting Started

Method 1: Download the IT Support App from Teams

The recommended way to get started is to install the IT Support app directly from the Microsoft Teams app store.

1 Open Microsoft Teams

Launch the Microsoft Teams application on your computer, mobile device, or open it in your web browser.

2 Go to Apps

Click on "Apps" in the left sidebar of Teams (look for the grid icon near the bottom of the sidebar).

3 Search for IT Support

In the search bar at the top, type "IT Support" and press Enter.

4 Select the App

Find "IT Support" in the search results (created by Dispatch Energy) and click on it to view the app details.

5 Review App Information

On the app details page, you can review the app features and permissions:

  • Bots: Complete tasks, find info, and chat using prompts
  • The app can receive and send messages, and access basic profile information
6 Add the App

Click the "Add" button to install the IT Support app. You may also see an option to add it to a specific team or chat.

7 Start Using IT Support

Once installed, the IT Support bot will appear in your chat list. Click on it to start a conversation and get help with your IT questions.

After installing, you can easily access IT Support anytime from your Teams chat list or by searching for it in the Teams search bar.

Method 2: Start a Chat Directly

If the app is already installed in your organization, you can start chatting with the bot directly.

1 Open Microsoft Teams

Launch the Microsoft Teams application on your computer or open it in your web browser.

2 Go to Chat

Click on "Chat" in the left sidebar of Teams.

3 Start a New Chat

Click the "New Chat" button (pencil icon) at the top of the chat list.

4 Search for the Bot

In the "To:" field, type "IT Support" and select it from the list that appears.

5 Start Chatting

You're ready! Simply type your question or describe your issue and press Enter.

If you can't find the IT Support app or bot, contact your IT department to ensure it has been installed for your organization.

3. Asking Questions

The easiest way to use the IT Support Bot is to simply ask your question in plain English, just like you would ask a coworker.

How to Ask for Help

1 Type Your Question

Describe your problem or question in the chat. Be specific about what's happening.

2 Read the Response

The bot will provide troubleshooting steps or information to help solve your issue.

3 Follow the Steps

Try the suggested solutions one at a time.

4 Provide Feedback

Click This Helped if the solution worked, or Still Need Help if you need more assistance.

Tips for Better Results

  • Be specific - "Outlook won't open" is better than "email broken"
  • Include error messages - If you see an error, include the exact text
  • Mention what you've tried - "I already restarted my computer"
  • One issue at a time - Ask about one problem per conversation
You don't need to use special commands or technical language. Just type naturally and the bot will understand!

4. Creating Support Tickets

When the bot's suggestions don't solve your problem, or if you need hands-on IT assistance, you can create a support ticket.

Method 1: Click "Still Need Help"

After the bot provides a solution, click the Still Need Help button. A ticket form will appear.

Method 2: Use the Ticket Command

Type the following command to open the ticket form directly:

/ticket

Filling Out the Ticket Form

Field What to Enter
Category Select the type of issue (Password, Software, Hardware, etc.)
Priority Low - Not urgent, can wait
Medium - Affects your work but has workaround
High - Significantly impacts your work
Critical - Complete work stoppage
Subject Brief description of the issue (e.g., "Cannot print to 3rd floor printer")
Description Detailed explanation of the problem, what you've tried, and any error messages
Please select the appropriate priority level. Marking all tickets as "Critical" delays response to actual emergencies.

After Submitting

You will receive a confirmation with your ticket number (e.g., IT-0042). Keep this number to check your ticket status later.

5. Checking Ticket Status

Want to know what's happening with your support request? Use the status command to check.

How to Check Status

Type the following command, replacing the ticket number with your own:

/status IT-0042

Understanding Ticket Status

Status What It Means
New Your ticket has been received and is waiting to be assigned
In Progress An IT team member is actively working on your issue
Awaiting User IT needs more information or action from you
Awaiting IT Waiting for IT resources or third-party assistance
Resolved The issue has been fixed - please verify it works for you
Closed The ticket has been completed and closed
If your ticket status is "Awaiting User," check your email or Teams messages - IT may have sent you a question or instructions!

6. Available Commands

While you can chat naturally with the bot, these commands provide quick access to specific features:

Command What It Does
/help
 Shows a list of available commands and how to use the bot
/ticket
 Opens the form to create a new support ticket
/status [ticket#]
 Checks the status of a specific ticket (e.g., /status IT-0042)
Commands start with a forward slash (/). Make sure there's no space before the slash!

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is the bot available 24/7?

Yes! The IT Support Bot is available around the clock. You can get instant help and create tickets anytime. However, tickets requiring human IT assistance will be addressed during business hours.

Q: Does the bot replace the IT Help Desk?

No, the bot works alongside your IT team. It handles simple, common issues instantly and creates tickets for complex problems that need human expertise.

Q: Can the bot access my computer or files?

No, the bot cannot access your computer, view your screen, or look at your files. It only provides instructions and guidance that you follow yourself.

Q: What if the bot's solution doesn't work?

Click the "Still Need Help" button or type /ticket to create a support ticket. A human IT team member will then assist you.

Q: How do I know if my ticket was received?

After submitting a ticket, you'll receive an immediate confirmation with a ticket number. You can also use /status [ticket number] to verify.

Q: Can I update a ticket I already submitted?

To add information to an existing ticket, simply mention your ticket number and the additional details when chatting with the bot, or reply to any email notifications about your ticket.

Q: What if I have an emergency IT issue?

For critical issues affecting multiple people or causing complete work stoppage, create a ticket with "Critical" priority. For life-safety emergencies, contact your building security or emergency services.

Q: Is my conversation with the bot private?

Your direct messages with the bot are between you and the IT system. However, ticket information is visible to IT staff who need it to resolve your issues.
